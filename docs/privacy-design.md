# Privacy design

Safe Nest treats referral, resident, safeguarding, domestic-abuse, housing, health-adjacent, and finance information as potentially sensitive. The design minimises public projections, keeps the CRM behind server-side authentication, separates finance notes from support notes, and applies restricted-record warnings and audit trails.

Public pages use only neighbourhood-level property information. The public API does not expose secure addresses, resident identities, room labels, capacity-by-room details, or confidential operational metadata. Email subjects and ordinary notifications contain references and next steps, not special-category details.

Before launch, the organisation must confirm lawful bases, privacy notices, data-sharing agreements, resident consent/communication practices, international transfer arrangements, retention periods, subject access procedures, safeguarding exemptions, and DPIA conclusions with responsible legal, safeguarding, and data-protection personnel.
