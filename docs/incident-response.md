# Incident response

1. Protect people first. In immediate danger use emergency services and the organisation's safeguarding escalation route.
2. Contain access: revoke sessions, disable accounts, rotate affected credentials, and restrict the affected record or storage prefix.
3. Preserve evidence: audit logs, provider delivery metadata, timestamps, and infrastructure logs. Do not copy unnecessary sensitive content into the incident ticket.
4. Assess scope with the safeguarding, data-protection, legal, and operational leads.
5. Notify affected people, regulators, partners, or law enforcement where the organisation's policy and law require it.
6. Recover from a verified backup or rollback, patch the cause, and test before restoring normal access.
7. Record decisions, actions, owners, times, and lessons learned. Review the retention and DPIA implications.

The organisation must approve contact trees, notification timelines, safeguarding escalation, and controller/processor responsibilities before production use.
